Qeet Pay aims to bring everything a business needs to move money — collect, bill, disburse,
invoice, and settle — into one API, one dashboard, and one integration, built India-first.

## Payment acceptance

Collect via **UPI** (collect, intent, QR), **cards** (3DS 2.0 with RBI tokenization), **net
banking**, and **wallets** — through hosted checkout, payment links, or an SDK. A single
payment object tracks state across rails.

## Payouts & disbursements

Send money out over **UPI / IMPS / NEFT**, individually or in bulk, with **maker-checker
approvals** and refunds — for vendor payments, salaries, or marketplace settlements.

## Subscription billing

Model recurring revenue: subscription **lifecycle and proration**, with **flat, per-unit,
tiered, and usage-based** pricing, collected through **NACH and UPI AutoPay** mandates, plus
rule-based dunning for failed collections.

## GST-compliant invoicing

Invoicing is GST-native: **CGST/SGST/IGST auto-calculation**, **HSN/SAC** lookup, credit and
debit notes, and **e-invoicing with IRN** — so invoices are compliant by construction, not by
a downstream reconciliation step.

## Marketplace & splits

**Split payments** across sellers with automatic **per-GSTIN GST attribution** and TCS
handling — the model marketplaces need to pay out correctly and stay compliant.

## India compliance

Compliance is foundational: **KYB onboarding**, **India data residency**, fraud scoring, and
the GST/e-invoicing obligations above. The roadmap sequences regulated capabilities behind
the approvals they require.

## Where this is heading

The phased plan — foundation rails first, then AI and deeper GST/compliance, then enterprise
and cross-border — is on the [roadmap](/pay/roadmap). Until Pay ships, [Qeet ID](/id)
provides the identity and tenant model the billing layer will build on.
