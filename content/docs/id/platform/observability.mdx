Qeet ID exposes the signals you need to run it in production: **Prometheus metrics**,
**OpenTelemetry tracing**, and health probes.

## Metrics

Prometheus-format metrics are served at `/metrics` for scraping.

<APIEndpoint method="GET" path="/metrics" title="Prometheus metrics" />

## Tracing

Qeet ID emits **OpenTelemetry** spans. Tracing is a **no-op until an OTLP endpoint is
configured** — point it at your collector (Tempo, Jaeger, Honeycomb, Datadog, …) to
turn it on. The SDKs propagate context so traces span your services and Qeet ID.

## Health probes

<APIEndpoint method="GET" path="/healthz" title="Liveness" />
<APIEndpoint method="GET" path="/readyz" title="Readiness (includes a DB ping)" />

Wire `/healthz` to your orchestrator's liveness probe and `/readyz` to readiness — the
latter fails until the database is reachable, so traffic isn't routed to a pod that
can't serve.

<Callout type="info">
  Combine these with the per-tenant [analytics overview](/docs/platform/multi-tenancy#analytics)
  for product KPIs (MAU/DAU, login/MFA/failed-login, method mix) alongside the
  operational metrics.
</Callout>
